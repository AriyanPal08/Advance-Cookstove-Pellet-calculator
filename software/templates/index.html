Methodology

How the Simulation Works

A transparent look at every input, every physics factor, and the real research behind the numbers.

The Problem

Biomass cookstove users currently guess pellet load and cook time, leading to wasted fuel, under-cooked meals, or dangerous overheating. The Tadka Chulha simulator replaces guesswork with physics.

This tool runs a 1 Hz transient thermal simulation — a discretized numerical model that steps through every second of the cooking process, tracking heat flow in and out of the system.

The Physics Model

Every second, the simulator computes: pellet combustion heat input, convective and radiative heat loss (Stefan–Boltzmann law), sensible heating of food, water, and vessel, and latent heat of water evaporation.

This is not a static formula — it's a full transient loop that tracks temperature rise, boiling onset, evaporation rate, and dry-boil conditions second by second.

Model architecture: 1 Hz loop with Euler-method integration. Vessel geometry from measured Indian cookware dimensions.

Every Input, Explained

Dish — Food mass, added water, specific heat capacity from Choi & Okos (1986), ICMR-NIN (2017) Indian Food Composition Tables, CSIR-CFTRI (2020), and Singh (2007) hydration kinetics.

Pellet type — Gross Calorific Value (GCV) range and combustion kinetics from Mukunda et al. (2010), IISc Bangalore gasifier data.

Utensil — Mass, material specific heat, emissivity from NIST WebBook, Incropera et al. (2007), MacCarty et al. (2010), Energy Sustain. Dev.

Ambient & Wind — Convective heat-transfer coefficient scaled by wind tier (Calm, Light, Moderate, Strong).

Choi & Okos, 1986 · ICMR-NIN, 2017 · Mukunda et al., 2010 · Incropera et al., 2007 · MacCarty et al., 2010

Lid & Pressure Cooker

Lid state — Open, covered, or sealed pressure cooker each dramatically change evaporation rate and heat loss.

Pressure cooker correction — An Arrhenius-kinetics-derived, empirically-calibrated correction for post-whistle cooking speed. This is currently validated against a limited number of physical tests and is still being refined.

Note: The pressure cooker model is under active calibration. Do not treat these estimates as fully validated — they are best-effort approximations pending further field testing.

Arrhenius kinetics model with empirical calibration factors. Field validation in progress.

The Output

The simulation produces: heat-up time to boiling, kinetic/cooking time for the recipe, a safety buffer scaled to thermal mass and wind conditions, and the total recommended cook time.

From the total time and combustion rate, the tool computes the exact pellet mass needed, with a procurement margin that varies by cooking mode (open pot vs. covered vs. pressure cooker).

Validation status: Lab-tested against a growing set of dishes. Field validation is in progress — accuracy numbers will be published as testing expands.

Safety buffer model accounts for thermal inertia, wind exposure, and vessel geometry.
